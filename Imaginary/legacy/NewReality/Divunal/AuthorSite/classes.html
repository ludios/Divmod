Common Divunal Classes

You can conserve memory and time in creating your objects by Extending them from a Class_Object. This causes them to inherit all of the properties of the Class_Object, except for Name, Description, and Aliases (other names). For example, you could make a wearable shirt by simply drawing a shirt, describing it, and extending it from Class_Shirt. That saves you the trouble of having to enable it to the Wear verb and assign all of the locations it covers (left arm, right arm, chest) yourself.

Character Classes

Class_Player: The basic "playable character" class, this includes verbs that all characters need to have... Look, Take, Drop, various emotes, etc. It also gives the character a password, and allows players who know that password to log in as that character.

Class_Human: An extension of Class_Player for human characters (the Gods of Divunal). This adds the various inherent Godly powers, like Gate and Scrutinize.

Room Classes

Class_Dark Room: This gives rooms the additional isLit property, and a number of event handlers which cause players who enter and leave the room to react accordingly. Dark Rooms remain dark until there is an object in the room (carried by a player, inside an open container, etc.) with an "isLit" property set to "true".

Class_Surface Room: (Not quite working yet) This defines the room as being on the surface of a body of water; Players will occasionally fail to move if they can't pass Swimming checks, and in the event of such a failure, they will often sink (be pushed through the "down" exit of the room, if available). A Surface Room can be used to represent a room with about 5' of water, but it can be made much deeper by adding a down exit to a Submerged Room.

Class_Submerged Room: (Not quite working yet) This defines a room as being completely underwater; Players in underwater rooms must pass a swimming test before they can move, and will pass out and drown after an ammount of time based on their Endurance. (Drowned characters will normally just take a bunch of health damage and wash up in some other room.

Object Classes
Class_Door: This makes a generic door, which can be used to obstruct an exit. It includes the Open/Close verbs, as well as default messages and aliases ("door", "doorway").

Class_SmallBook: Small Books gain a simple Read verb, along with a "book text" string that will be returned to the character when the book is read. This is meant more for short messages, notes, or summaries of complete books ("This is a dull, meandering account of the history of rice production in a country referred to as Northwestern Iadeonesia... it is so tedious and pointless that you can't bring yourself to keep reading it."

Class_Container: Containers are Locations, and include a verb which allows players to Get/Put objects in and out of them.

Class_Closeable Container (Not quite working yet) A container, with an additional verb for opening and closing it. (You cannot look at or take objects in closed containers.)

Class_Book: Books inherit the TomePage and TomeWrite verbs, which allow the creator to enter in a large string for each page ("write 1 in book" will opena dialogue to write page 1), and let other players read the book (reading the book will display the current page, which can be selected with "turn next in book" or "turn prev in book". Clothing Classes

Class_Clothing: A generic piece of clothing. It can be worn and removed, but you need to add the locations it covers yourself.

Class_Gloves: A clothing item which covers the hands.

Class_Shirt: A piece of clothing that covers the chest and arms.

Class_Socks: A piece of clothing that covers the feet.

Class_Pants: A piece of clothing that covers the legs.

Class_Tunic: A piece of clothing which covers the chest.

Class_Cape: A piece of clothing that hangs off the neck.

Class_Necklace: A piece of clothing that is worn on the neck.

Class_Shoes: A piece of clothing that is worn on the feet (but is too big to be worn under other similar clothing).

Class_Cloak:A piece of clothing that covers the body from below the neck to just above the feet.
